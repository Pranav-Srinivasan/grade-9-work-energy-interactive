4. Power - The Rate of Doing Work

Deriving Time from Power

1 Work done against gravity: W = F √ó h = Weight √ó Height
2 Power is work per unit time: P = W / t
3 Rearranging for time: t = W / P = (Weight √ó Height) / Power

5. Test Your Understanding

Question: The Tired Statue Puzzle

You hold a heavy suitcase above your head for 30 minutes. You're sweating and exhausted. How much work did you do on the suitcase according to Physics?

A) A lot - I'm exhausted!
B) 30 √ó weight of suitcase
C) Zero Joules
D) Cannot be determined
Correct! Zero Joules.

Work = Force √ó Displacement
Since the suitcase didn't move (displacement = 0), no work was done on it!

Your muscles are tired because of physiological processes (converting chemical energy), not because you did physical work on the suitcase. This is the difference between "feeling tired" and "doing work" in physics!
